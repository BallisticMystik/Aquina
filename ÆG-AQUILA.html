06 — Framework
Hydration Logic · Mineral Palette

The rules behind every response.

Deterministic frameworks, not vibes. Duration, sweat, and mineral chemistry drive every plan.

— Hydration Framework
Duration < 60 min

Plain water is usually sufficient unless it is very hot, humid, or the user sweats heavily.

60 – 120 min

Water plus electrolytes — especially sodium — may be useful for longer or sweat-heavy sessions.

2+ hours

Endurance hydration must be planned with fluid, sodium, and carbohydrate together.

Heavy sweater

Heavy sweat, salt marks on clothing, or repeated cramps in heat elevate electrolyte importance.

Warning — Hyponatremia

Over-reliance on plain water during long endurance events is a real risk. Electrolytes are protective, not optional.

07 — Safety
Hard Rules · Red Flags · IP

Safety is non-negotiable.

Hard rules, red-flag protocols, and IP protection are compiled into every output. No diagnosis, no cures, no exceptions.

  • General education only — no diagnosis, treatment, or prescriptions.
  • Always refer to medical professional: kidney disease, heart disease, blood pressure, diabetes, pregnancy, diuretics.
  • Never recommend extreme water intake.
  • Never claim water or electrolytes cure disease.
  • No detox, alkaline-cure, or unsubstantiated medical performance claims.
  • Warn about hyponatremia risk during long endurance events with high plain-water intake.
  • Espresso machine profiles must always carry scale and corrosion validation warning.
  • Food-grade minerals only — always state this explicitly.

⚠ Red-Flag Symptoms

  • Confusion or disorientation
  • Fainting or loss of consciousness
  • Chest pain
  • Persistent vomiting
  • Severe weakness

→ Direct immediately to emergency medical care. Do not continue hydration guidance.
